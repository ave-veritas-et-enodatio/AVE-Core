## Section 2.1: The Implosion Paradox: Why The Vacuum Must Be Micropolar

A classical Cauchy elastic solid supports transverse waves only under MacCullagh's 19th-century condition ($\lambda = -\mu$). However, the bulk modulus of a Cauchy solid is $K = \lambda + \frac{2}{3}\mu$. Substituting MacCullagh's condition yields:

$$
K = -\mu + \frac{2}{3}\mu = -\frac{1}{3}\mu
$$

A negative bulk modulus means any density fluctuation causes runaway contraction --- the vacuum would instantly implode into a singularity. This thermodynamic catastrophe forces a fundamental constraint: the vacuum substrate *cannot* be a classical Cauchy solid. It must be a **Chiral LC (Micropolar) Continuum**, where independent rotational degrees of freedom (microrotations) decouple transverse wave propagation from the volumetric bulk modulus, permitting $K > 0$ simultaneously with pure transverse gauge boson propagation.
