## The CMB as an Asymptotic Thermal Attractor

The continuous injection of latent heat into the photon gas (Cosmic Microwave Background) forms a permanent asymptotic thermal floor. By modelling the universe as a standard radiation network ($P = \frac{1}{3}\rho$) with a continuous volumetric generative source term driven by the latent heat of lattice crystallisation ($\Gamma = \frac{1}{V}\frac{dQ}{dt} = 3H\rho_{latent}$), the cosmological continuity equation evaluates to:

> **[Resultbox]** *Cosmological Continuity Equation*
>
> $$
> \dot{\rho}_{rad} + 4H\rho_{rad} = 3H\rho_{latent}
> $$

Converting this differential equation to evaluate against the cosmological scale factor ($a$), the system integrates against standard adiabatic expansion cooling ($a^{-4}$) to yield:

> **[Resultbox]** *Radiation Energy with Latent Floor*
>
> $$
> u_{rad}(a) = U_{hot}a^{-4} + \frac{3}{4}\rho_{latent}
> $$

As $a \to \infty$, the standard adiabatic expansion cooling ($a^{-4}$) is offset by the continuous latent heat injection. The temperature smoothly asymptotes to the fundamental Unruh-Hawking temperature limit ($T_U \sim 10^{-30}\text{ K}$), resolving the thermodynamic Heat Death paradox.
