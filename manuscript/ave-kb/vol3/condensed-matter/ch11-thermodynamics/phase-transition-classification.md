## General Classification of Phase Transitions

All classical phase transitions map onto impedance matching events in the LC lattice:

| **Transition** | **Impedance Change** | **Physical Manifestation** |
|---|---|---|
| Solid $\to$ Liquid | $Z_{shear} \to 0$ | Shear modulus vanishes |
| Normal $\to$ Superconducting | $Z_{eff} \to 0$ | Perfect phase-lock ($R = 1$) |
| Paramagnetic $\to$ Ferromagnetic | $Z_\mu \to 0$ locally | $\mu_{eff}$ saturates, domains align |
| Bose-Einstein Condensation | $|\Gamma| \to 1$ globally | Macroscopic coherent reflection |
| Deconfinement (QGP) | $Z_{int} \to Z_0$ | Impedance matching dissolves confinement |

The critical temperature $T_c$ for each transition is the temperature at which the RMS thermal noise amplitude exceeds (or falls below) the impedance mismatch threshold required to maintain (or break) a particular structural resonance. Phase transitions are not spontaneous symmetry breaking in an abstract order-parameter field. They are mechanical impedance catastrophes in a physical elastic medium.
