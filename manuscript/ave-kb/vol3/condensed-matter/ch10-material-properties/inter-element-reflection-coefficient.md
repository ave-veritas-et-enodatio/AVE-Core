> **[Resultbox]** *Inter-Element Reflection Coefficient*
>
> The reflection coefficient between two elements $i$ and $j$ at a bond interface is:
>
> $$\Gamma_{ij} = \frac{Z_{atom,j} - Z_{atom,i}}{Z_{atom,j} + Z_{atom,i}}$$
>
> Elements within the same impedance family ($|\Gamma_{ij}| \to 0$) bond efficiently because their vibrational modes couple resonantly. Elements across families ($|\Gamma_{ij}| \sim 0.5$) experience strong reflection at the interface---the signature of a reactive, ionic bond. This framework predicts:
>
> - **Metallic bonding** (intra-family): Carbon--Carbon, Silicon--Silicon, and Magnesium--Aluminum pairs show $|\Gamma| < 0.03$, explaining covalent and metallic lattice formation.
> - **Ionic bonding** (inter-family): Sodium ($Z_{atom} = 1.454$) paired with Nitrogen ($Z_{atom} = 0.379$) yields $|\Gamma| = 0.59$---a strong reflective mismatch that manifests as ionic character.
> - **Noble inertness**: Helium and Neon possess zero magnetic susceptibility and closed-shell impedances, resulting in $|\Gamma| \approx 0$ with each other but no external coupling hooks---explaining their refusal to form bonds despite low reflection coefficients.
