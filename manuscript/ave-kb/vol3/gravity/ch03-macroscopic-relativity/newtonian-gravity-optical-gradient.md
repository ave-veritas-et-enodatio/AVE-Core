> **[Resultbox]** *Newtonian Gravity from Optical Gradient*
>
> $$
> F_{grav} = -\frac{GM m_i}{r^{2}}\hat{r}
> $$

Taking the spatial gradient of the ponderomotive wave energy directly yields the gravitational acceleration, expressed as $F_{grav} = -\nabla U_{wave}$.

Because the localised wave energy is defined by the particle's inductive inertia $m_i$, it cancels out of the acceleration equation ($F = ma$), guaranteeing that inertial mass and gravitational mass are identical ($m_i \equiv m_g$).
