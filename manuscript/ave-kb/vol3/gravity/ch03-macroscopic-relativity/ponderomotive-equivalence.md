## The Ponderomotive Equivalence Principle

Standard physics invokes the Weak Equivalence Principle ($m_i = m_g$) as an axiomatic postulate. AVE derives it from macroscopic wave mechanics.

Because a massive topological wave-packet acts as a 3D isotropic defect, it couples to the spatial volume via the $1/7$ Lagrangian isotropic projection (derived in Chapter 4). The effective scalar refractive index perceived by mass is $n_{scalar}(r) = 1 + \epsilon_{11}(r)/7 = 1 + GM/c^{2}r$. The localised stored energy of the knot is its internal inductive rest mass ($m_i c^{2}$) scaled inversely by the refractive density:

> **[Resultbox]** *Ponderomotive Wave Energy*
>
> $$
> U_{wave}(r) = \frac{m_i c^{2}}{n_{scalar}(r)} \approx m_i c^{2}\left(1 - \frac{GM}{rc^{2}}\right) = m_i c^{2} - \frac{GM m_i}{r}
> $$

Taking the spatial gradient directly yields the gravitational acceleration, expressed as $F_{grav} = -\nabla U_{wave}$:

> **[Resultbox]** *Newtonian Gravity from Optical Gradient*
>
> $$
> F_{grav} = -\frac{GM m_i}{r^{2}}\hat{r}
> $$

Because the localised wave energy is defined by the particle's inductive inertia $m_i$, it cancels out of the acceleration equation ($F = ma$), guaranteeing that inertial mass and gravitational mass are identical ($m_i \equiv m_g$).
