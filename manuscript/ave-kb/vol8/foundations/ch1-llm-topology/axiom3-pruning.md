# Axiom 3: Least Reflected Action in Pruning

Axiom 3 states that systems organize to minimize reflected action, typically captured by the return loss coefficient $\Gamma$. The decision to apply a discrete binary phase-transition mask (buckled vs conductive) rather than a continuous scale factor mitigates catastrophic reflection ($\Gamma$) across the surviving topology.

The reflection coefficient generated by pruning the buckled neurons (where $r \ge 1.0$) is mathematically modeled as an impedance discontinuity between the intact and culled states:

$$
\Gamma_{\text{prune}} = \frac{Z_{\text{pruned}} - Z_{\text{original}}}{Z_{\text{pruned}} + Z_{\text{original}}}
$$

Where $Z$ represents the cumulative energetic capacity ($\Sigma A^2$) of the layer. Acceptable culling strategies must maintain $|\Gamma|^2 \ll 0.25$ to preserve transitive structural cohesion.
