# The Thermodynamic Emergence of $A_c$

In physical lattices, the critical boundary condition $A_c$ emerges from Hamiltonian mechanics (e.g., $T_c$ in BCS superconductivity arises from phonon coupling equilibria). In the virtual LLM lattice, $A_c$ emerges directly from the training dynamics.

At convergence, the gradient descent process establishes a generic thermal equilibrium across the weight manifold. Thus, the logical yield limit of the domain corresponds to the mean distributed energy of the parameter space:

$$
A_c^2 \equiv \overline{A^2} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{j=1}^{N} A_j^2
$$

This establishes the zero-free-parameter foundation for structural culling without empirical thresholds.
