# Resolution of the Breakdown Paradox

The Z-A inversion resolves the apparent paradox in topological pruning limits. Both biological and virtual systems fail when the normalized strain $r$ exceeds the universal breakdown boundary $r \geq 1.0$. However:

- **Biological Breakdown**: Excitotoxicity occurs when $Z$ becomes excessively low, permitting lethal signal volume.
- **Virtual Buckling**: Network collapse occurs when $\|w\|$ (and thus $Z$) exceeds the mathematically tolerated bounds, buckling the virtual manifold.

Axiom 4 remains universally invariant; the topology buckled, but the directional operator linking impedance to amplitude reversed.
