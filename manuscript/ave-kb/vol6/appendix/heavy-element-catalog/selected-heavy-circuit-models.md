## Equivalent Circuit Models of Selected Heavy Elements

The following circuit diagrams depict the alpha-cluster subcircuit architecture for each of the six heavy elements above. Each alpha macro-block ($\alpha$) encapsulates a synchronized 4-nucleon LC mesh. Bus lines represent the 16 cross-coupled inductive pairs ($4 \times 4$ mutual links) between adjacent alpha blocks.

[Figure: circuit_s32.pdf --- see manuscript/vol_6_periodic_table/figures/]

**Sulfur-32**: $8\alpha$ Cube. First Large Signal element ($M = 32.8$, $V_R/V_{BR} = 0.994$). 496 discrete nucleon-nucleon connections.

[Figure: circuit_ar40.pdf --- see manuscript/vol_6_periodic_table/figures/]

**Argon-40**: $10\alpha$ Bicapped Square Antiprism. Noble gas with complete $n=3$ closure. 780 connections.

[Figure: circuit_ca40.pdf --- see manuscript/vol_6_periodic_table/figures/]

**Calcium-40**: $10\alpha$ Large Signal ($M = 32.9$). Same topology as Ar-40 but avalanche multiplication required.

[Figure: circuit_ti48.pdf --- see manuscript/vol_6_periodic_table/figures/]

**Titanium-48**: $12\alpha$ Cuboctahedron. First transition metal. 1128 connections across 66 inter-alpha pairs.

[Figure: circuit_cr52.pdf --- see manuscript/vol_6_periodic_table/figures/]

**Chromium-52**: $13\alpha$ Centered Icosahedron. Central alpha coupled to 12-vertex shell. 1326 connections.

[Figure: circuit_fe56.pdf --- see manuscript/vol_6_periodic_table/figures/]

**Iron-56**: $14\alpha$ FCC-14 architecture. Peak binding energy per nucleon. 1540 connections across 91 inter-alpha pairs.
