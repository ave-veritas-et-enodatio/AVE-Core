## Radioactive Decay as Impedance Mismatch

In classical discrete electrical engineering, when an AC geometric bridge or LC network fails to properly couple (yielding a critically low $Q$-factor), the system reflects wave energy and experiences destructive internal tension. Applied to topological nuclear physics, this explicitly drives radioactive isotope decay.

When unstable isotopes are modeled using the AVE mutual impedance simulator, their localized geometries inherently prevent the formulation of a highly resonant, stable core.

### Tritium ($^3$H) Beta Decay

Tritium ($1p, 2n$) lacks the necessary geometric symmetry to fold into a tight topological knot. The solver proves that to match its empirical mass defect ($8.48$ MeV), its nodes must be stretched to a wide $\sim 3.5d$ separation. This results in a critically low Topological $Q$-factor of just $3.20$. To eliminate this extreme parasitic strain, the topology spontaneously ejects a unit of phase (an electron via $\beta$-decay) to transition into the stable Helium-3 ($^3$He) lattice, which boasts a tight, highly symmetrical $Q=19.52$ footprint.

The empirical mass-energy difference for this transition is $m({}^3\text{H})c^2 - m({}^3\text{He})c^2 \approx 0.529$ MeV, of which the $\beta$-endpoint kinetic energy is $\sim 18.6$ keV (the rest going to the electron rest mass and the antineutrino). The framework's contribution at this leaf is *mechanistic* — the $Q$-factor optimization ($3.20 \to 19.52$) explains *why* the transition occurs spontaneously and which direction it runs; the energy *magnitude* is taken from empirical mass-energy data, not derived independently here. (An attempt to derive the magnitude from the framework's pairwise coupling $K \cdot \sum(1/r_{ij})$ across the two geometries is tracked as future work in the active burndown.)

### Beryllium-8 ($^8$Be) Alpha Fission

Conversely, the Beryllium-8 geometry ($4p, 4n$) consists of two massive $^4$He Alpha tanks but fundamentally lacks the critical central bridging neutron required to establish mutual inductance ($M_{bridge}$) between them. As an open Wheatstone bridge with zero central coupling, the two macro-components instantly repel and cleanly shatter back into independent Alpha fragments.
