# Helium (Z=2)

Helium-4 (the Alpha particle) forms the first perfectly symmetrical closed topological knot shell. Two $6^3_2$ protons and two neutrons interlock into a tetrahedral macro-knot that minimizes external geometric strain. This geometry acts as a polyphase resonant transformer with astronomical $Q$-factor ($Q > 19$), defining chemical inertness (Noble Gas). Helium-4 is the irreducible reference material for the semiconductor binding engine---its intra-alpha binding energy defines $V_{BR}$.

## Key Results

| Result | Expression |
|---|---|
| Alpha particle mass | $M(^4\text{He}) = (2m_p + 2m_n) - 6(K/(d_{\text{core}}\sqrt{8})) = 3727.379$ MeV |
| Binding energy (mass defect) | $\Delta m(^4\text{He}) = 6(K/(d_{\text{core}}\sqrt{8})) \approx 28.30$ MeV |
| Mutual coupling constant | $K = \frac{5\pi}{2} \cdot \frac{\alpha \hbar c}{1 - \alpha/3} \approx 11.337$ MeV$\cdot$fm |
| Q-factor | $Q > 19$ (maximum theoretical; extreme stability) |
| Breakdown voltage | $V_{BR} = 6\,\alpha\hbar c / D_{\text{intra}} \approx 3.631$ MeV |
| Orbital radius | $r_{He} \approx a_0 / 1.70$ (shielded by $Z_{eff} \approx 1.70$) |
| Semiconductor classification | Single alpha ($1\alpha$): reference material, not a device; $V_R/V_{BR}$ undefined |
