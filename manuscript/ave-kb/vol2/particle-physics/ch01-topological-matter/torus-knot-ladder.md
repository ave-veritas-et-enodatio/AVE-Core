## The Torus Knot Phase Winding Ladder

The $(2,q)$ torus knots classify the phase winding number of stable topological defects. Each odd crossing number $q$ defines a unique soliton confinement radius $r_{opt} = \kappa_{FS} / q$, where $\kappa_{FS} = 8\pi$ is the cold Faddeev--Skyrme coupling constant. The ladder is:

| $(2,q)$ Knot | $q$ | $r_{opt}$ | Gauge Symmetry | Particle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Trefoil ($3_1$) | 3 | $8.317\,l_{node}$ | SU(2) | Electron |
| Cinquefoil ($5_1$) | 5 | $4.990\,l_{node}$ | SU(3) | Proton (938 MeV) |
| $7_1$ knot | 7 | $3.564\,l_{node}$ | SU(4) | $\Delta(1232)$ |
| $9_1$ knot | 9 | $2.772\,l_{node}$ | SU(5) | $\Delta(1620)$ |
| $11_1$ knot | 11 | $2.268\,l_{node}$ | --- | $\Delta(1950)$ |
| $13_1$ knot | 13 | $1.919\,l_{node}$ | --- | $N(2250)$ |

The crossing number $q$ constrains the phase gradient by absorbing a fraction of the total coupling. Higher $q$ produces smaller, more tightly wound solitons with correspondingly higher mass. The proton's cinquefoil ($q = 5$) gives $r_{opt} = \kappa_{FS}/5 \approx 4.99\,l_{node}$. All values use $\kappa_{FS} = 8\pi(1 - 1/(14\pi^2))$ from `ave.core.constants` with zero empirical fits.
