## Regime Classification of Topological Matter

Every stable particle in the AVE framework operates within the universal regime landscape defined by the Axiom 4 saturation kernel. The regime boundary determines whether the particle's internal field stresses remain within the linear elastic response of the lattice or enter the non-linear saturation plateau.

| Particle | Topology | Regime | Physical Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| Photon ($\gamma$) | Propagating wave | I (Linear) | $\Delta\phi \ll \alpha$; free propagation |
| Neutrino ($\nu$) | Twisted unknot | I--II boundary | Chiral phase below yield |
| Electron ($e^-$) | $0_1$ Unknot | II (Yield) | $\Delta\phi = \alpha$; self-confinement |
| Proton ($p$) | $(2,5)$ Cinquefoil | II (Yield) | Borromean linkage at saturation |
| W/Z Bosons | Transient defect | III (Rupture) | $\Delta\phi > \alpha$; temporary |
| Black Hole | Macroscopic soliton | III (Rupture) | $V_{topo} \gg V_{yield}$; metric collapse |
