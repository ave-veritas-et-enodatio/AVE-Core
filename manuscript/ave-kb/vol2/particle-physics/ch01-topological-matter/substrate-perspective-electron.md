# What the Substrate "Sees" of the Canonical Electron

The substrate-perspective operational view of the canonical electron: NOT field-theorist's view (Cosserat $\omega$-field on horn-torus unknot etc.), but **what each lattice node locally experiences** when a canonical electron exists in the substrate. The lattice doesn't "see an electron" — it sees: a localized region of high $A^2$ saturation; a self-formed TIR wall at the loop boundary; a topologically conserved circulation pattern; a circulating current generating B-flux; a long-range refractive-index tail (gravitational mass); and a coupled K4-Cosserat field structure. **Charge, mass, spin, magnetic moment, gravitational coupling, Compton wavelength** are all EMERGENT readings of the substrate's joint state at the canonical electron configuration — not extra properties added on top.

## §1 — What each lattice node has (substrate observables, per Axiom 1)

### K4 sector (capacitive + inductive on each bond)

For each of 4 ports (tetrahedral connectivity per Axiom 1):

| Observable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| $V_{\text{inc}}[\text{port}]$ | Incoming voltage on bond LC's C-state |
| $V_{\text{ref}}[\text{port}]$ | Reflected (outgoing) voltage on bond LC's C-state |
| $\Phi_{\text{link}}[\text{port}]$ | Integrated flux on bond LC's L-state (conjugate to $V_{\text{inc}}$ per A-010) |

Bond LC dynamics: C-state ($V_{\text{inc}}$) and L-state ($\Phi_{\text{link}}$) trade off in time; bond stores reactive energy $\tfrac{1}{2} C V^2 + \tfrac{1}{2} L \Phi^2 / L^2$.

### Cosserat sector (rotational + translational microstructure)

| Observable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| $u$ | Translation displacement (3-vector per node) |
| $\omega$ | Rotation displacement (3-vector per node, SO(3)-valued) |
| $\varepsilon$ | Strain tensor (symmetric $\nabla u$ + cross-coupling with $\omega$) |
| $\kappa$ | Curvature tensor ($\nabla \omega$) |

$\omega$ is the substrate microrotation per node. The SO(3) → SU(2) double cover at the substrate level gives spin-½ character to any spinor observable extracted from $\omega$. **The Cosserat microrotational DOF IS the substrate-native origin of intrinsic spin** (per Axiom 1 canonical character).

### Op14 saturation observables (Axiom 4, derived per-cell)

| Observable | Definition | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| $A^2_{\text{local}}$ | $(\sum_{\text{ports}} V_{\text{inc}}^2) / V_{\text{SNAP}}^2$ | Per-cell aggregate saturation level (chi-squared-of-4 across ports) |
| $S_{\text{local}}$ | $\sqrt{1 - A^2_{\text{local}}}$ | Saturation factor (Born-Infeld kernel $n = 2$) |
| $Z_{\text{eff}}$ | $Z_0 / \sqrt{S}$ | Effective impedance (modulated by saturation) |
| $c_{\text{eff}}$ | $c_0 \cdot \sqrt{S}$ | Effective wave speed |
| $n_{\text{eff}}$ | $c_0 / c_{\text{eff}} = 1/\sqrt{S}$ | Effective refractive index (gravitational analog per Vol 3 Ch 2) |

### Coupled K4-Cosserat saturation (full Axiom 4 mixing)

When both K4 $V_{\text{inc}}$ and Cosserat $\omega$ are non-zero, the saturation couples both:

$$A^2_{\text{total}} = A^2_V + A^2_\omega$$

where $A^2_V = (V_{\text{inc}}^2 + V_{\text{ref}}^2) / V_{\text{SNAP}}^2$ and $A^2_\omega = \kappa^2 / \omega_{\text{yield}}^2$.

This is the substrate-level coupling channel (Op14 cross-block) that connects K4 sector dynamics to Cosserat sector dynamics.

## §2 — What the substrate "sees" when a canonical electron is present

The canonical electron is a $0_1$ unknot (real-space curve) carrying a Beltrami standing wave with SU(2) bundle structure at horn-torus geometry $R = r = \ell_{\text{node}}/(2\pi)$.

### At the loop's tube core ($\rho_{\text{tube}} \approx 0$)

| Observable | State |
|---|---|
| $\|\omega\|^2$ | **Large** — Beltrami standing wave concentrated here per hedgehog envelope |
| $A^2_\omega$ | **High** — close to or above Op14 saturation threshold $\sqrt{2\alpha} \approx 0.121$ |
| $S_{\text{local}}$ | **Small** — saturation engaged |
| $Z_{\text{eff}}$ | **High** — local impedance $\gg Z_0$ |
| $c_{\text{eff}}$ | **Small** — wave propagation slow here |
| $n_{\text{eff}}$ | **Large** — local refractive index high (local clock slowest) |

**The tube core is a region of trapped, saturated, slowed substrate.**

### At the loop's surface (Regime II boundary)

| Observable | State |
|---|---|
| $A^2_{\text{local}}$ | Crosses $\sqrt{2\alpha}$ from above to below moving outward |
| $\lvert\nabla A^2\rvert$ | **Large** (sharp gradient from saturated to vacuum) |
| $\lvert\nabla Z_{\text{eff}}\rvert$ | Correspondingly large — sharp impedance step |
| Local $\Gamma^2$ | High per Op3 ($\Gamma \approx \tfrac{1}{2} \nabla \ln Z_{\text{eff}}$) |
| TIR-wall behavior | Incoming waves from outside (low $Z$) bounce off inside (high $Z$) surface; trapped waves bounce going outward |

**The loop's surface is a self-formed Total Internal Reflection wall** — the field that's there creates the saturation gradient that creates the wall that traps the field. **Self-maintaining circular causation.**

### Outside the loop (Regime I, vacuum)

| Observable | State |
|---|---|
| $\|\omega\|^2$, $\|V_{\text{inc}}\|^2$ | Decay rapidly (hedgehog tail; exponentially-suppressed in saturation regime) |
| $A^2_{\text{local}}$ | $\approx 0$ — vacuum |
| $S_{\text{local}}$ | $\approx 1$, $Z_{\text{eff}} \approx Z_0$, $c_{\text{eff}} \approx c_0$ |

But **not perfectly** — there's a long-range effect ($1/r^2$ via Op14 coupling per Vol 3 Ch 3 macroscopic-relativity):
- **Local clock slowing** persists at long range as $O(GM/rc^2)$ — gravitational analog
- **Refractive-index gradient** — light bends around the saturated region (gravitational lensing analog, weakly)

### In the loop's interior (Regime III plasma, if deep saturation)

For the canonical electron unknot at horn torus ($R_{\text{loop}} = r_{\text{tube}}$), there's **no "interior"** — the loop tube IS the structure. Inside the tube, the field is saturated; outside, it's vacuum. The loop is self-contained.

For OTHER structures (e.g., trefoil torus knot for proton $5_1 / 5_2$), there might be a meaningful interior with separate Regime III plasma physics. But for the canonical electron unknot, no interior exists.

## §3 — How the substrate "reacts" (dynamics)

The lattice doesn't have a special "electron mode" or "electron equation." It has **saturating LC dynamics** that the canonical electron CONFIGURES into a topologically stable pattern. The reactions are the substrate's standard dynamics evaluated on that pattern.

### Local scattering (Axiom 1 substrate)

At each node, $V_{\text{inc}}$ waves arrive on each port; the scatter S-matrix sends out $V_{\text{ref}}$ on each port. With saturation engaged:
- $Z_{\text{eff}}$ modulates the S-matrix coefficients per port
- Bond LC tanks store reactive energy as $\Phi_{\text{link}}$ grows
- Energy is conserved per Axiom 3 effective action (no dissipation in closed-system limit)

Where field is high (loop core), scatter is heavily modulated. Where field is low (vacuum), scatter is near-trivial.

### Self-maintaining trap

The saturation IS where the field is. The field IS where the saturation traps it. This is **not chicken-and-egg** — it's a **self-organized fixed point** of substrate LC dynamics:

- Remove the field locally → saturation drops → $Z_{\text{eff}}$ drops to $Z_0$ → trap dissolves → undisturbed substrate has no localized structure
- Have field but no saturation (linear regime) → field disperses → no localization → no electron
- **Both needed simultaneously**: field amplitude high enough to engage saturation, saturation patterned enough to trap field. The unknot topology is the simplest stable solution.

This is **Axiom 3 (Minimum Reflection Principle) at the operational level**: the substrate's least-reflected-action principle stabilizes the configuration where field + saturation jointly minimize action. **The unknot is the topological ground state of that principle.**

### Topological conservation

The unknot's $c = 0$ topology is a TOPOLOGICAL invariant. Continuous deformations of the field cannot change $c$ without crossing a barrier:

- **Charge conservation** (per Axiom 2 TKI: $[Q] \equiv [L]$; unknot's circumference IS the unit charge)
- **Electron can't decay to vacuum** — would need to cross a topological barrier
- **Two unknots can annihilate** — deform into pair-of-loops → one big loop → two opposite-sign loops → vacuum (pair-creation/annihilation kinematics; see [pair-production-axiom-derivation](pair-production-axiom-derivation.md))

The lattice REACTS to these topological constraints via substrate's continuity equations + saturation dynamics. **No special "topology operator" needed.**

### Magnetic moment generation

The unknot has circulating $\omega$-field around the loop. **Closed current loop**:
- B-flux threads loop interior (perpendicular to loop plane)
- External B-field exerts torque on loop's magnetic moment
- Result: gyroscopic precession (Larmor frequency $\omega_L = \gamma B_0$)

This isn't quantum mechanical magic — it's **classical Newton's-laws-of-rotation applied to the loop's angular momentum** per [spin-gyroscopic-isomorphism](spin-gyroscopic-isomorphism.md).

**The $g$-factor $= 2$** emerges from the SU(2) bundle: SO(3) $\omega$-field has period $2\pi$, but spinor observable has period $4\pi$ → ratio 2 → $g = 2$.

### Op14 long-range coupling (gravitational analog)

In Regime I outside the loop, substrate is locally "vacuum" but there's a faint refractive-index gradient persisting at long range (Op14 saturation kernel propagates outward via $1/r^2$ coupling):

- **Light bending around the electron** (gravitational lensing at electron-scale; tiny but nonzero)
- **Time dilation in electron's vicinity** (clock at radius $r$ runs slower by $O(GM/rc^2)$ — substrate's local clock-rate modulation per A-010)
- **Mutual electron interaction** — two electrons feel each other's refractive-index gradients → effective inverse-square attraction at long range (**gravitational mass**)

**The electron's mass** shows up as a long-range gravitational-coupling consequence of the local saturation kernel. Per Vol 3 Ch 3 macroscopic-relativity: gravity IS this Op14-saturation refractive-index gradient propagated to macroscopic scales.

### K4-Cosserat coupling via Op14 (when both sectors non-zero)

The canonical electron has structure in BOTH sectors:
- Cosserat $\omega$: Layer 1 + Layer 2 (real-space unknot + SU(2) bundle)
- K4 $V_{\text{inc}} / V_{\text{ref}}$: Layer 3 (phase-space $(2, 3)$ winding on Clifford torus)

Op14 couples them via $A^2_{\text{total}} = A^2_V + A^2_\omega$. When both sectors have field, saturation is TOTAL, and the trap involves BOTH:
- Cosserat $\omega$ circulates around the loop in real space
- K4 $V_{\text{inc}} / V_{\text{ref}}$ phasor traces $(2, 3)$ winding in phase space
- The two patterns are coupled at the substrate via Op14
- Together they form the FULL canonical electron

## §4 — Operational summary: substrate's "view"

The lattice doesn't "see an electron." It sees:

1. **A localized region of high $A^2$ saturation** — high local impedance, slow local clock, $n_{\text{eff}} \gg 1$
2. **A self-formed TIR wall at the boundary** — sharp $Z_{\text{eff}}$ gradient confining field
3. **A topologically conserved circulation pattern** — closed loop, $c = 0$, can't decay
4. **A circulating current** — generates B-flux, magnetic moment, $g = 2$ via SU(2) bundle
5. **A long-range refractive-index tail** — gravitational mass, light bending, clock slowing in vicinity
6. **A coupled K4-Cosserat field structure** — Layer 1 + 2 in Cosserat $\omega$, Layer 3 in K4 $V_{\text{inc}} / V_{\text{ref}}$

| Gravitational coupling | Op14 long-range saturation tail |

None of these are "extra" properties added on top of substrate's local observables. They are **EMERGENT readings of the substrate's joint state at the canonical electron configuration**.
