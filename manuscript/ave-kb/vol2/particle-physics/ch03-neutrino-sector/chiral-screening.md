## Step 1: Chiral Screening Threshold

The SRS/K4 lattice is chiral (Chapter 3, Section 2) and 3-connected: each node has exactly 3 bonds. The chiral dispersion relation ($\omega^2 = c^2 k^2 \mp \gamma_c k$) acts as a high-pass filter on torsional wave coupling (Section 2).

To couple two torsional modes with crossing number difference $\Delta c = |c_i - c_j|$, the lattice must transfer $\Delta c$ units of torsional angular momentum. Each K4 bond transfers at most 1 unit per interaction. With 3 bonds per node, the maximum single-interaction transfer is:

> **[Resultbox]** *Chiral Screening Threshold*
>
> $$
> \Delta c_{\text{crit}} = 3 \qquad \text{(K4 connectivity = trefoil crossing number)}
> $$

Modes with $\Delta c \le 3$: the compliance channel is **open** (bulk coupling permitted).
Modes with $\Delta c > 3$: the compliance channel is **screened** (evanescent --- same mechanism as right-handed neutrino parity violation).

Note: The screening threshold $\Delta c_{\text{crit}} = 3$ is simultaneously the K4 lattice connectivity, the trefoil crossing number, and the number of Cosserat sectors. These three facts are structurally identical: the trefoil has $c = 3$ crossings *because* the K4 lattice is 3-connected.
