## Step 3: Perturbative Junction Corrections

The junction coupling $Y_{\text{junc}} = 1/(c_1 c_3) = 1/45$ persists as an evanescent tail beyond the chiral screening boundary, adding perturbatively to each mixing angle:

> **[Resultbox]** *PMNS Mixing Angles*
>
> $$
> \begin{align}
>     \sin^2\theta_{13} &= \frac{1}{c_1 c_3} = \frac{1}{45} \\[4pt]
>     \sin^2\theta_{12} &= \nu_{vac} + \frac{1}{c_1 c_3} = \frac{2}{7} + \frac{1}{45} = \frac{139}{450} \\[4pt]
>     \sin^2\theta_{23} &= \frac{1}{2} + \frac{2}{c_1 c_3} = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{2}{45} = \frac{49}{90}
> \end{align}
> $$

The factor of 2 in $\theta_{23}$ arises because the middle mode ($c_2 = 7$) has two adjacent junction paths (coupling toward $c_1$ and toward $c_3$), doubling the effective junction admittance. This is a parallel admittance sum: $Y_{\text{total}} = Y_{\text{compliance}} + n \cdot Y_{\text{junction}}$, where $n$ is the number of independent junction paths.
