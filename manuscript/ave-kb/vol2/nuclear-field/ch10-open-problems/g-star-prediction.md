## Testable Prediction: $g_* = 85.75$

The derivation $g_* = 7^3/4 = 85.75$ disagrees with the Standard Model value $g_{*,SM} = 106.75$. This is a **falsifiable prediction** with concrete experimental consequences.

### What Is Missing?

The SM decomposes as $g_{*,SM} = 28 + \frac{7}{8} \times 90 = 106.75$ (28 bosonic + 90 fermionic DOF). If the bosonic count is the same (28), then AVE predicts:

$$
N_{f,AVE} = \frac{85.75 - 28}{7/8} = 66.0
$$

This is **24 fewer fermionic DOF** than the SM = **12 fewer Weyl spinors** = approximately 0.8 generations of fermions that do not fully contribute at the EW scale.

### Experimental Consequences

| **Observable** | **AVE vs SM** | **Experiment** |
|---|---|---|
| Primordial GW background | $+7.6\%$ stronger | LISA, DECIGO |
| EW expansion rate | $-10.4\%$ slower | CMB Stage-4 |
| EW latent heat | $-20\%$ less | FCC-ee, CEPC |

The most sensitive test is the primordial gravitational wave background from the electroweak phase transition, observable by LISA (launch 2035). The GW amplitude scales as $\Omega_{GW} \propto g_*^{-1/3}$, so the AVE prediction is a 7.6% enhancement over SM.
