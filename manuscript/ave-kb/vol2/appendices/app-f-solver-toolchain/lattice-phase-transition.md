## Lattice Phase Transition: Solid $\to$ Fluid

The phase transition at $\varepsilon_{11} = 1$ is the gravitational analogue of the solid--liquid transition:

| Property | $\varepsilon_{11} < 1$ (elastic) | $\varepsilon_{11} > 1$ (ruptured) |
|---|---|---|
| Shear modulus $G$ | $G > 0$ (solid lattice) | $G = 0$ (topology melted) |
| Group velocity $c_g$ | $c \cdot (1 - \varepsilon^2)^{1/4}$ | 0 (frozen) |
| Phase velocity $c_\phi$ | $c / n(r)$ | $\to \infty$ ($n \to 0$) |
| Transverse waves | Propagate (GW) | **Cannot propagate** |
| Longitudinal waves | Propagate | May propagate |
| Analogue | Crystalline solid | Fluid (no shear) |

This phase transition also explains why the interior of the saturated region acts as a perfect conductor for GW modes: the QNM is a *surface wave* at the elastic--ruptured phase boundary, identical in character to a Stoneley wave at a solid--fluid interface.
