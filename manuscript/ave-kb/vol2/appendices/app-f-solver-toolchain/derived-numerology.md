## Derived Numerology

### The Universal Eigenvalue

Every domain-specific eigenvalue is a single evaluation of one universal formula:

> **[Resultbox]** *Universal Regime-Boundary Eigenvalue*
>
> $$
> \omega \cdot r_{\mathrm{char}} = \frac{\ell\,(1 + \nu_{\mathrm{vac}})}{x_{\mathrm{sat}}}
> $$

where $\ell$ is the topological mode number, $\nu_{\mathrm{vac}} = 2/7$, and $x_{\mathrm{sat}}$ is the saturation number in units of the domain's characteristic length. **All validated eigenvalues are instances of this one expression:**

| Domain | $\ell$ | $x_{\mathrm{sat}}$ | Value | Error |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BH QNM ($\omega_R M_g$) | 2 | 7 | $18/49 = 0.3673$ | 1.7% vs GR |
| Proton QNM ($\omega D_p/c$) | 5 | 1 | $45/7 = 6.4286$ | $-0.8\%$ vs $N(1520)$ |

| Protein backbone ($f/f_0$) | 7 | 1 | $63/7 = 9.000$ | $+0.1\%$ vs IR |
