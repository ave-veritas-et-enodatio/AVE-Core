## The Quartz Cylinder

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Material | X-cut $\alpha$-quartz | Piezoelectric, ultralow loss |
| Diameter | 50 mm | Standard resonator size |
| Length | 50 mm | Near $\lambda/2$ at $f_{res} \approx 59.6$ kHz |
| $\varepsilon_r$ | 4.5 | Low permittivity $\to$ high field penetration |
| $\tan\delta$ | $10^{-5}$ | Negligible thermal dissipation |
| Electrodes | Sputtered Cr/Au | Ohmic contact, no air gap |
| AC frequency | 50 kHz | Piezoelectric resonance |
| AC amplitude | 500 V peak | Standard ultrasonic driver |
| DC bias | 30 kV | $68.7\%$ of $V_{yield}$ |

The quartz is driven near its fundamental thickness-mode resonance ($f_{res} \approx 59.6$ kHz for a $50\text{ mm}$ cylinder at $v_s = 5960$ m/s). The AC drive uses a standard ultrasonic piezo driver. The $30\text{ kV}$ DC bias is supplied by a laboratory HV power supply through a large blocking inductor ($> 1\text{ H}$) to prevent AC current from flowing into the DC supply.
