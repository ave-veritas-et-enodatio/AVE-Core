## Bistatic Parallax Offset

One method to mitigate the cumulative thermal load limitation while preserving the experimental signal is to operate in a **Synchronized Dual-Drive (Bistatic) Mode**. By using one array purely as a high-power fractional-duty emitter and a separated, unpowered secondary array as a passive parallax receiver, the total integrated thermal burden on the system is halved. The secondary array detects the structural reaction mass (the $\tau_{zx}$ wake) purely via induced $S_{11}$ detuning, entirely avoiding active dielectric dissipation on the receiving end.
