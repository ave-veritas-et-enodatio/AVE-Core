# Ch.7 Topological SMES

A macroscopic Superconducting Magnetic Energy Storage (SMES) device routed as a $(p,q)$ torus knot synthesizes a confined Beltrami force-free field ($\nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \lambda \mathbf{B}$, $\mathbf{J} \parallel \mathbf{B}$), eliminating Lorentz self-destruction and reducing external stray flux by 87.9% compared to a solenoidal design.

## Key Results

| Result | Statement |
|---|---|
| Force-free condition | $\nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \lambda \mathbf{B} \implies \mathbf{J} \times \mathbf{B} = 0$ (zero structural tension) |
| Stray flux reduction | 87.9% leakage reduction vs. solenoid ($(150,3)$ torus knot, Biot-Savart) |
