## Metric-Catalyzed Fusion Scaling Laws

The impact of active metric compression on fusion parameters follows directly from the Topo-Kinematic Identity and the Axiom-4 saturation kernel. Let $n_{scalar}$ denote the local effective refractive index (lattice density enhancement). Three scaling laws follow:

> **[Resultbox]** *Metric-Catalyzed Fusion Scaling Laws*
>
> $$
> \begin{align}
> r(n) &= \frac{a_0}{n_{scalar}} \\[6pt]
> T_{ign}(n) &= \frac{T_0}{n_{scalar}^{2}} \\[6pt]
> V_{topo}(n) &= \frac{V_{topo,0}}{n_{scalar}^{3}}
> \end{align}
> $$

The effective Bohr radius shrinks inversely with the metric compression, directly analogous to the muon-catalyzed radius reduction.

**Derivation of $V_{topo}(n)$:** The collision strain is $V_{topo} = F/\xi_{topo}$, where $F = E_k/d_{turn}$ is the average Coulomb deceleration force. In a metrically compressed space with $c_{local} = c_0/n$, the turning distance at the Gamow-shifted kinetic energy $E_k(n) = E_0/n^2$ is:

$$
d_{turn}(n) = \frac{\alpha\hbar c_{local}}{E_k(n)} = \frac{\alpha\hbar (c_0/n)}{(E_0/n^2)} = n \times d_{turn,0}
$$

The turning distance *increases* because the kinetic energy ($\propto 1/n^2$) drops faster than the coordinate compression ($\propto 1/n$). The collision force therefore decreases as $F(n) = E_k(n)/d_{turn}(n) = F_0/n^3$.
