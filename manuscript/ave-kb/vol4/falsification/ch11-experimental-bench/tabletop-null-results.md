### Vacuum-Flux Drag Test (VFDT)

If $\mathbf{A} \equiv \mathbf{p}_{vac}$, a 50 kA EMP toroid should drag a laser beam via Fresnel-Fizeau. However:

- $p_{vac} = \Phi \cdot \xi_{topo} \approx 1.30 \times 10^{-8}$ kg·m/s
- Vacuum mass in torus core: $M_{vac} = \rho_{bulk} \times V \approx 97{,}450$ kg
- Drift velocity: $v_{vac} \approx 1.33 \times 10^{-13}$ m/s
- Phase shift: $\sim 10^{-14}$ rad — **undetectable**

This null result is *required* — if a 50 kA magnet could drag the vacuum at 1 cm/s, the spatial metric inside an MRI machine would visibly warp light.

### Regenerative Vacuum Receiver (RVR)

A spinning Tungsten lobe next to an LC tank circuit pumps the local vacuum parameters:

- Modulation depth: $\delta_L = G \cdot m / (c^2 r) \approx 7.4 \times 10^{-26}$
- Required Q for oscillation: $Q \geq 2.7 \times 10^{25}$
- Best cryogenic SRF Q: $\sim 10^{11}$
- Shortfall: **15 orders of magnitude**

**Rule**: Scalar gravity tests fail on a tabletop because they are fatally suppressed by the $G/c^2$ scalar gap.
