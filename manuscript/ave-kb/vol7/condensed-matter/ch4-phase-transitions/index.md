# Ch.4: Phase Transitions

This chapter applies the AVE saturation operator and LC lattice model to phase transitions in water and fluid flow. The water melting point is derived as a proton transfer eigenmode, the H--O--H bond angle as an impedance eigenvalue, and the water density anomaly as topological cell collapse. Turbulence onset is identified with the Regime I $\to$ II boundary crossing.

## Key Results

| Result | Statement |
|---|---|
| Water melting point | $T_m = T_0 \cdot S(r_0) = 273.46$ K ($+0.14\%$); bare $T_0 = 279.5$ K corrected by Axiom 4 saturation |
| H--O--H bond angle (Op3) | $\cos\theta_\text{HOH} = -1/4$, $\theta_\text{HOH} = \arccos(-1/4) = 104.48°$ (exp: $104.45°$, error: $+0.03°$) |
| H--O--H bond angle (Op8) | $\cos\theta_\text{HOH}^{(\text{large})} = -\pi\sqrt{2}/18 \approx -0.2468$, $\theta = 104.29°$ (error: $-0.16°$) |
| Topological cell collapse | $V_{II} = V_I \times \varphi_{FCC} = V_I \times \pi\sqrt{2}/6 \approx 23.11$ A$^3$; liquid water density 1005.8 kg/m$^3$ ($+0.7\%$) |
| Turbulence onset | Regime I $\to$ II boundary at $r_1 = \sqrt{2\alpha} \approx 0.1208$; saturation operator $S(\text{Re}, \text{Re}_c) = \sqrt{1 - (\text{Re}/\text{Re}_c)^2}$ |
| Water condensation | Saturation-driven phase transition: $S(T, T_{\mathrm{dew}}) = \sqrt{1 - (T_{\mathrm{dew}}/T)^2}$ |
