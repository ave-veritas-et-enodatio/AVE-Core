# Ch.3: Superconductivity

This chapter derives superconductivity as a classical phase-locked gear train of topological inductive flywheels (electrons as $3_1$ knots). The Meissner effect emerges from the monolithic moment of inertia of trillions of interlocked gyroscopes, and room-temperature superconductivity is predicted via nanoscale Casimir cavity acoustic filtering.

## Key Results

| Result | Statement |
|---|---|
| London penetration depth isomorphism | $B(x) = B_0 e^{-x/\lambda_L} \Longleftrightarrow \omega(x) = \omega_0 e^{-x/\lambda_{\text{inertial}}}$ |
| Room-temperature Casimir superconductivity | Casimir cavity acts as acoustic high-pass filter for vacuum metric; Kuramoto phase-lock at 300 K achieved in $N=500$ shielded array ($R = 1$) vs. open-field failure ($R \approx 0$) |
