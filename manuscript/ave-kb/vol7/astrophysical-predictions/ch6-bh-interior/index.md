# Ch.6: Regime IV — The Black Hole Interior

This chapter applies the six-step AVE methodology to the only universal regime with zero prior engine validation: Regime IV (Ruptured Topology). The analysis reveals a fundamental structural distinction between the black hole interior and particle confinement, despite both being instances of $S(A/A_c) = 0$. The BH is the only Regime IV system with symmetric saturation ($Z = Z_0$, $\Gamma = 0$).

## Key Results

| Result | Statement |
|---|---|
| BH interior as DC operating point | Gravity is a matched transmission line ($Z = Z_0$ everywhere); BH interior is the region past dielectric breakdown where $G_{shear} = 0$ |
| Saturation radius | $r_{sat} = 7GM/c^2$; $r_{sat}/r_s = 7/2 = 3.50$ (exact) |

| Regime IV isomorphism | Four systems at $S = 0$: electron (asym, $\Gamma = -1$), superconductor (asym, $\Gamma = -1$), nucleus (asym, $\Gamma = +1$), BH (sym, $\Gamma = 0$) |

| Information paradox resolution | BH interior is a dissipative sink; information absorbed by ruptured lattice |
| Hawking radiation | Gravitational Schwinger effect at $r \approx r_{sat}$ where $G_{shear} \to 0$ |
| BH merger ringdown | Validated to 1.7% via 5-step eigenvalue method ($\omega \cdot M = 0.3673$ vs. GR exact $0.3737$) |
