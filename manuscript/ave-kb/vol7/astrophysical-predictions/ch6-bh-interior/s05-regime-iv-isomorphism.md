# Step 4: The Regime IV Isomorphism

All four Regime IV systems use $S(A/A_c) = 0$, but they differ
in *which* constitutive parameter saturates:

> **[Resultbox]** *Regime IV Isomorphism Table*
>
> | **System** | **Saturates** | **Sym** | $Z$ | $\Gamma$ | **Interior** | **EE Analog** |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Electron | $\mu$ (inductive) | Asym | $\to 0$ | $-1$ | Standing wave | LC tank |
> | Superconductor | $\mu_{eff}$ | Asym | $\to 0$ | $-1$ | Standing wave | Shorted $L$ |
> | Nucleus (Pauli) | $U(r)$ at $d_{sat}$ | Asym | $\to\infty$ | $+1$ | Repulsive wall | Open circuit |
> | BH interior | $G_{shear}$ | **Sym** | $= Z_0$ | $0$ | Dissipative sink | Past $V_{BR}$ |

**The BH is the ONLY Regime IV system with no impedance mismatch.**
The electron is a *knot* (topology wound up);
the BH is a *hole* (topology torn apart).
Both use the same saturation operator, but the symmetry of saturation
determines whether the interior is a resonant cavity or a dissipative sink.
