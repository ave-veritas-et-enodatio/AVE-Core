# Klopfenstein Reservoir Profile

The characteristic impedance of a parallel-plate waveguide is:

$$Z = \sqrt{\frac{L'}{C'}}$$

where the per-unit-length parameters are:

$$L' = \mu \frac{d}{w}, \qquad C' = \varepsilon \frac{w}{d}$$

Combining:

$$Z = \sqrt{\frac{\mu}{\varepsilon}} \cdot \frac{d}{w} = Z_{mat} \cdot \frac{d}{w}$$

To create a low-impedance energy storage cavity, the waveguide width $w$ is expanded by a factor $\beta = w_{\rm res}/w_0$:

$$Z_{\rm res} = Z_0 / \beta$$

The transition from the standard trace to the expanded reservoir uses a Klopfenstein taper profile to eliminate reflections at the entry and exit planes. This ensures energy flows smoothly into and out of the reservoir without generating parasitic standing waves.

> **[Resultbox]** *Chapter 7 Summary*
>
> The Strain Reservoir is a Klopfenstein-tapered wide-section waveguide engineered to provide low characteristic impedance ($Z_{\rm res} = Z_0/\beta$) while preserving phase velocity. It replaces the classical capacitor: energy is stored as elastic metric strain within the Axiom 4 yield boundary.
