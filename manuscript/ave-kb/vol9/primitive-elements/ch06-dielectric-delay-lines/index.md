# Ch.6: Dielectric Delay Lines (Slow-Wave Structures)

Derives that Dielectric Corrugation Slow-Wave Structures follow from Axiom 1 alone. The Telegraphist equations establish that phase velocity $v_{ph} = 1/\sqrt{L'C'}$. Periodic corrugations of pitch $\Lambda$ and depth $d$ increase the effective per-unit-length inductance and capacitance, slowing the wave without introducing dispersion within the operating band.

## Key Results

| Result | Statement |
|---|---|
| Telegraphist Wave Equation | $\partial^2 V/\partial x^2 = L'C'\,\partial^2 V/\partial t^2$ |
| Phase Velocity | $v_{ph} = 1/\sqrt{L'C'}$ — retarded by corrugation geometry |
| Group Velocity Preservation | Within the passband, group velocity matches phase velocity (no dispersion) |
