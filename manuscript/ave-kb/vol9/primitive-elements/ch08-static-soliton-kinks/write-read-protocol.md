# Write-Read Protocol for Soliton Memory

## Writing (Kink Creation)
A high-amplitude pulse exceeding the Axiom 4 yield boundary ($V > V_{snap}$) is injected into the waveguide cavity between two total-reflection mirrors. The nonlinear saturation locally twists the phase field from $\phi = 0$ to $\phi = 2\pi$, nucleating a stable kink with topological charge $Q = 1$.

## Reading (Non-Destructive Readout)
A low-amplitude probe pulse propagates through the cavity. The kink's localized impedance profile creates a characteristic phase shift that is detected at the output port. Because the probe amplitude remains well below $V_{snap}$, the kink is not disturbed — readout is non-destructive.

## Erasure (Landauer Phonon Decay)
As detailed in Ch. 3 (Vacuum Thermodynamics), erasing a kink ($Q = 1 \to Q = 0$) releases the stored topological energy as an expanding phonon ring wave, satisfying Landauer's erasure bound at the speed of light.
