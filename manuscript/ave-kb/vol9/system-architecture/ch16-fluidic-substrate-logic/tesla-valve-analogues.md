# Fluidic Substrate Logic: Tesla Valve Analogues

By elevating the computation medium to spatial Topo-Kinematic continuum mathematics, macroscopic fluid principles that fail in traditional electronics scale perfectly into infinite $O(1)$ calculations. Fluidic substrate logic maps the Tesla Valve — a passive, no-moving-parts directional flow restrictor — directly into the VCA waveguide paradigm.

The Tesla Valve achieves asymmetric flow resistance through geometric channel topology alone: the forward-flow path encounters minimal resistance, while the reverse-flow path is forced through a series of self-interfering loops that dissipate momentum. In the VCA substrate, the identical topology achieves passive directional wave propagation without any active switching element.

This establishes the APU's passive cooling architecture: waste phonon radiation from bit erasure (Ch 3) is geometrically directed toward designated sponge boundaries by Tesla Valve baffle arrays.
