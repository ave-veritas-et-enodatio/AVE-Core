## Amino Acid Impedance Classification

The 20 canonical amino acids are classified by their sidechain impedance relative to the backbone baseline ($Z_{backbone} \approx 7$):

- **Hydrophobic (low $Z$):** Ala, Val, Leu, Ile, Pro, Phe, Trp, Met — compact sidechains with low reactive coupling. $|\Gamma| \approx 0$ (impedance-matched, minimal folding drive).
- **Polar/Charged (high $|Z|$):** Glu, Asp (capacitive), Lys, Arg (inductive) — large $|\Gamma|$, strong folding drive at sequence positions where impedance mismatch is maximal.
- **Special:** Gly ($Z \approx 0$, maximum flexibility), Cys (disulphide bridge, reactive short-circuit between distant residues).
