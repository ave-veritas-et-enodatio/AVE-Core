## Mass → Inductance: $L = m / \xi^2$

In AVE, the atomic nucleus is a tightly bound, high-density topological knot in the $M_A$ lattice. This knot provides localized rotational inertia. In electrical terms, inertia is defined as **Inductance** ($L$). The dimensional transduction via $\xi^2$ yields:

> **[Resultbox]** *Topological Inductance of an Atom*
>
> $$
> L_\text{atom} \;=\; \frac{m_\text{atom}}{\xi^2_\text{topo}}
> \qquad [\text{Henries}]
> \label{eq:L_atom}
> $$

This is derived directly from Axioms 1--2; no scaling factors are needed. The CODATA atomic masses serve as the only measured input. Table tab:inductances lists the resulting values for the core organic elements:

| **Element** | **Mass (Da)** | **Inductance (fH)** | **$\bar{\lambda}_C$ (fm)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hydrogen (H) | 1.008 | 9.72 | 1,320 |
| Carbon (C) | 12.011 | 115.9 | 110.8 |
| Nitrogen (N) | 14.007 | 135.1 | 95.0 |
| Oxygen (O) | 15.999 | 154.3 | 83.1 |
| Sulfur (S) | 32.065 | 309.3 | 41.5 |

*Atomic inductances derived from $L = m / \xi^2$. No free parameters.*
