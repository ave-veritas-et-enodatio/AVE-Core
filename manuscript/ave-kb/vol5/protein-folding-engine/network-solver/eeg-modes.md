# EEG Frequency Bands as Cortical Cavity Modes

The five canonical EEG bands ($\delta, \theta, \alpha, \beta, \gamma$) may correspond to the fundamental and harmonic standing wave modes of the cortical cavity:

$$f_n = \frac{n \cdot v_\text{axon}}{2 L_\text{eff}}, \qquad n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$$

where $L_\text{eff}$ is the effective round-trip cortical path length.

## EEG Bands as Cortical Cavity Modes

Using $v_\text{axon} \approx 5$ m/s (mean unmyelinated cortical velocity), $L_\text{eff} \approx 2.5$ m (cortical path length including folding).

| **Band** | $n$ | $f_\text{pred}$ (Hz) | $f_\text{obs}$ (Hz) | **Interpretation** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $\delta$ | 1 | 1.0 | 0.5--4 | Fundamental mode (sleep) |
| $\theta$ | 2 | 2.0 | 4--8 | First harmonic (memory encoding) |
| $\alpha$ | 5 | 5.0 | 8--13 | Idle mode (relaxed wakefulness) |
| $\beta$ | 10 | 10 | 13--30 | Active processing |
| $\gamma$ | 20 | 20 | 30--100 | Binding / consciousness |
