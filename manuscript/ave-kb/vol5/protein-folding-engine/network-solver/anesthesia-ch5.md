# Anesthesia as Broadband Impedance Mismatch

General anaesthetics may work by introducing a **global impedance perturbation** that prevents standing wave formation across the cortical network. The mechanism maps to Axiom 4 saturation:

- Anaesthetic molecules partition into lipid bilayers, changing membrane permittivity $\varepsilon_m$ and thus axonal $Z$.
- A uniform shift in $Z$ across all segments does *not* change relative $\Gamma$ between segments (impedance ratios are preserved).
- However, a *non-uniform* shift (agents preferentially partition into myelin vs. axoplasm) destroys the impedance matching at nodes of Ranvier, preventing coherent standing waves.
- Brainstem and spinal reflexes are preserved because they operate on shorter, lower-$Q$ transmission lines where impedance perturbation does not destroy the fewer resonant modes.

## Falsifiable Prediction

The minimum alveolar concentration (MAC) of an anaesthetic should correlate with its lipid--water partition coefficient *times* the impedance mismatch it introduces at the node of Ranvier, not simply with lipid solubility alone.
