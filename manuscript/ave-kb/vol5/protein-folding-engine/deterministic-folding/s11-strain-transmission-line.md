# SPICE Transmission Line Mismatch ($S_{11}$ Strain)

To formally prove that organic geometry is driven by electrical resonance, the exact amino acid sequence is modelled as a cascaded SPICE AC transmission line. By running a broad frequency sweep across the discrete R-group topologies, the macroscopic impedance mismatch (effectively the $S_{11}$ Reflection Coefficient) of the entire molecular chain is calculated.

*(Figure: **Topological AC Impedance Means Test:** A cascaded SPICE simulation of 10-residue polypeptide chains. The Alpha-Helix forming Polyalanine drops into a deep resonant notch (an impedance match), meaning the structure can "lock" into a tight helical wrapper without breaking. Conversely, Polyglycine and Polyproline exhibit large geometric mismatch (high reactive strain), causing the backbone to unwind and flatten into a Beta-Sheet or extended coil.)*

As shown in the figure, the macroscopic AC strain dictates the physical stability of the structure. Sequences with matched resonances (low $S_{11}$) remain tightly folded, while mismatched sequences (high $S_{11}$) reject the geometry.
