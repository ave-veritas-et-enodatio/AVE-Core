## Electromechanical Transduction Constant $\xi_\text{topo}$
<!-- id: lm9b3j -->

- $\xi_\text{topo} \equiv e/\ell_\text{node} = e\,m_e\,c/\hbar \approx 4.149 \times 10^{-7}$ C/m
- _Specific Claims_
  - $\xi_\text{topo}$ is the universal electromechanical coupling constant of the lattice. Its square $\xi^2$ is the dimensional bridge between mechanical (mass, stiffness) and electrical (inductance, capacitance) quantities for biological circuit modelling.
  - The constant is composed entirely of axiom-level constants ($e$, $m_e$, $c$, $\hbar$) — **no free parameters**.
  - Per CLAUDE.md INVARIANT-C2, $\xi_\text{topo}$ is the canonical bridge used in Vol 2 (atomic orbital mappings), Vol 4 (circuit engineering), and Vol 5 (mass-to-inductance, bond-stiffness-to-capacitance). The canonical definition leaf is `molecular-foundations/organic-circuitry/electromechanical-transduction-constant.md`.
- _Specific Non-Claims and Caveats_
  - Does NOT claim $\xi_\text{topo}$ is independently measured; it is a derived combination of CODATA-known constants.
  - $L_\text{atom} = m_\text{atom}/\xi^2$ and $C_\text{bond} = \xi^2/k_\text{bond}$ are **definitional** dimensional bridges (category i identities under the Master Prediction Table classification) — they convert mechanical inputs into electrical units, not numerical predictions. The downstream **predictions** (FTIR transfer functions, batch SPICE notches, folding RMSDs) are what the bridge enables; the bridge itself is not a falsifiable result.

## Hydrogen Bond Canonical Values $d_\text{HB}$, $E_\text{HB}$
<!-- id: j9l3ww -->

- $d_\text{HB} = 1.754$ Å; $E_\text{HB} = U_\text{raw} \times (1-\phi) = 0.832\,\text{eV} \times 0.2595 = 0.2158\,\text{eV} = 4.98$ kcal/mol
- _Specific Claims_
  - The H-bond is **not** a Fabry–Perot eigenvalue (no electrons shared) — it is an **Op4 pairwise interaction** between partial charges $q_\text{eff} = \Gamma e$ generated by the O–H polarity ($\Gamma = 1/3$).
  - $d_\text{HB}$ is the minimum of the Op4 potential $U(r) = -K_\text{HB}/r \cdot (1 - |\Gamma(r)|)$ with $K_\text{HB} = \Gamma^2 \alpha\hbar c$ and $d_\text{sat}^\text{HB} = r_H + r_O$ (atomic contact, NOT soliton overlap as in covalent bonds).
  - $E_\text{HB}$ is the raw Op4 well depth $U_\text{raw} = 0.832$ eV projected by the FCC void fraction $(1-\phi) = 1 - \pi\sqrt{2}/6 \approx 0.2595$, yielding 4.98 kcal/mol vs the gas-phase water-dimer reference $5.02 \pm 0.05$ kcal/mol ($<0.9\%$).
  - Per CLAUDE.md INVARIANT-C3, $(d_\text{HB}, E_\text{HB})$ are the canonical AVE H-bond values; Vol 3 references them without re-derivation.
  - O–O nearest-neighbour distance $d_\text{OO} = d_\text{OH} + d_\text{HB} = 2.727$ Å vs NIST ice I$_h$ 2.76 Å ($-1.2\%$).
- _Specific Non-Claims and Caveats_
  - Does NOT claim agreement with **bulk-liquid** H-bond energies ($\sim 5.5$ kcal/mol). The 4.98 kcal/mol target is the **isolated gas-phase water-dimer** intrinsic baseline; the additional energy in bulk arises from cooperative multi-cavity dipole enhancement, not from this calculation.
  - The void-fraction projection $(1-\phi)$ is justified by the H-bond routing through the interstitial vacuum gap rather than through occupied lattice. This is a structural/geometric argument; treat as the AVE interpretation of why the projection factor is exactly $(1-\phi)$, not as an independent measurement.
  - Does NOT claim derivation of the $+4^\circ$C water density anomaly from a scalar Boltzmann distribution. The leaf explicitly warns against that ("Scalar Transition Collapse"); the derivation requires the cooperative 3D directional matrix limit (Axiom 4 lattice yield) and surfaces in the membrane-yield calculation, not in $E_\text{HB}$ itself.
  - Bond eigenvalue table at the same chapter shows systematic errors of $-2$ to $-4$% (single bonds) and isolated outliers (S–S at $+30\%$); these are different bonds, not the H-bond. Do not conflate the H-bond's $<0.9\%$ accuracy with the covalent eigenvalue table accuracies.

> **Leaf references:** `molecular-foundations/organic-circuitry/hbond-op4-equilibrium.md`. Cross-volume invariant: CLAUDE.md INVARIANT-C3.

## Bond Eigenvalue $d_\text{eq}$ — Fabry–Perot Loaded Cavity
<!-- id: yyhczl -->

- $d_\text{eq} = \sqrt{2}\,\sqrt{r_A \cdot r_B} \times \sqrt{T^2(N_\text{eff})/T^2(2)}$ with $T^2(N) = 4N/(1+N)^2$ and $N_\text{eff} = 2(\text{Bond Order}) + \max(N_{s_A}, N_{s_B})/2$
- _Specific Claims_
  - Covalent bond distance is the **eigenvalue** of a loaded Fabry–Perot cavity (round-trip phase condition), not the minimum of an empirical potential surface. The $\sqrt{2}$ prefactor arises from the MCL two-electron loading $T^2(2) = 8/9$, not from geometry.
  - All inputs are first-principles: atomic port impedances $r_A, r_B$ from the MCL solver, atomic numbers, bond order, and the integer $N_\text{eff}$. No empirical force constants, IR frequencies, bond lengths, or Slater screening constants enter.
  - Verification: H$_2^+$ at $d_0 = 2a_0 = 1.058$ Å (matches exact QM); H$_2$ at $\sqrt{2}\,a_0 = 0.748$ Å vs NIST 0.741 Å ($+1.0\%$).
  - Coupled with topological projections (3-phase WYE balance for H terminals, $\pi$-coupling $4/9$, lone-pair $\cos^2(109.5^\circ) = 1/9$, polar $\pi$-slip, split-core transformer for period-3 atoms), 11 biological force constants are reproduced to $\leq 10\%$.
- _Specific Non-Claims and Caveats_
  - Errors are not uniformly small. Single bonds between two heavily-loaded shells (C–C, C–N, C–O at $-2$ to $-4\%$ in $d$) show systematic underprediction; the leaf flags this as "open refinement" requiring degenerate mode hybridisation.
  - S–S is an outlier at $+30\%$ in $d_\text{eq}$ (2.66 Å vs NIST 2.05 Å); $k$ at $230$ vs $236$ N/m ($-3\%$) is fine but the geometry is wrong. Treat S–S as **not yet validated** by this framework, not as quietly acceptable.
  - The C–H eigenvalue is $-7\%$ ($1.02$ vs $1.09$ Å), N–H is $-5\%$ ($0.96$ vs $1.01$ Å), C–N is $-4\%$. These are within the leaf's stated $\leq 10\%$ band but are not sub-percent agreements.
  - Does NOT claim a Pauling-style empirical bond-length parameterisation; the input audit is explicit that no bond-length table is consulted.

> **Leaf references:** `molecular-foundations/organic-circuitry/first-principles-bond-force-constants.md`.

## Batch SPICE — 18/20 Amino Acids at 1192 cm$^{-1}$
<!-- id: 4jy0t8 -->

- _Specific Claims_
  - Native MNA solver applied to first-principles SPICE netlists for all 20 standard L-amino acids produces a **shared primary absorption notch at 1192.1 cm$^{-1}$** for 18 of 20 amino acids (depths $-73$ to $-79$ dB), with Valine at 1343.9 cm$^{-1}$ and Glycine at 2819.1 cm$^{-1}$.
  - The clustering is a structural claim: the shared $\alpha$-carbon backbone topology dominates the macro-impedance, while R-groups attach as shunt stubs that load the junction node without shifting the primary series resonance.
  - Glycine's anomalously high resonance is a quantitative parameter-free explanation for its known anomalous flexibility in protein folding: vanishing R-group inductance ($L_H \approx 9.7$ fH vs $L_C \approx 116$ fH) lets the backbone resonate at higher frequency, producing a natural impedance discontinuity (a "hinge").
  - Computation has **zero adjustable parameters** when used together with the first-principles force constants of `first-principles-bond-force-constants.md`; if the force constants are taken from NIST IR rather than first principles, the framework's combined transfer function remains a genuine prediction but individual bond frequencies are inputs (the leaf is explicit about this partial circularity in `bond-stiffness-to-capacitance.md`).
- _Specific Non-Claims and Caveats_
  - The 1192 cm$^{-1}$ value is asserted as falling in the "amide fingerprint region" but the specific predicted frequency is **not** an absolute match to any single named amide-band centre — treat the cluster location as the structural prediction, not the precise value.
  - The "shared backbone" interpretation depends on R-groups loading as shunt stubs. This is a topological-circuit identification; bonds within R-groups that would change topology (e.g., Cys disulphide bridges between residues) are not modelled in the per-residue circuit.
  - Does NOT claim per-residue side-chain absorption fingerprints. The model resolves backbone passband behaviour, not site-specific side-chain stretches.

> **Leaf references:** `molecular-foundations/organic-circuitry/batch-spice-20-amino-acids.md`, `molecular-foundations/organic-circuitry/simulation-results-zero-parameter.md`, `molecular-foundations/organic-circuitry/ftir-falsification-test.md`.

## FTIR Falsification — 10/11 Peaks Within Predicted Passband
<!-- id: oilm45 -->

- _Specific Claims_
  - For Glycine and Alanine independently, 10 of 11 known NIST FTIR absorption peaks fall within the predicted backbone passband ($|H|^2 > -60$ dB) — measured against a **fixed** frequency scale with no parameter tuning.
  - The single failing peak in each case lies in the predicted high-frequency rolloff zone where the single-backbone-unit transfer function does not resolve individual bond stretches.
- _Specific Non-Claims and Caveats_
  - Does NOT claim per-peak frequency match. The criterion is **passband membership** ($|H|^2$ above threshold at the experimental peak frequency), not peak alignment.
  - The transfer function describes power transmission through the entire backbone, **not** local absorption at each bond site. A peak that fails passband membership is not necessarily a model failure for that bond; it can also reflect impedance mismatch between R-group stub and main chain.
  - Self-consistency check (e.g., C–H stretch at 3003 cm$^{-1}$ vs experimental $\sim 3000$ cm$^{-1}$) is **definitional** ($f = (1/2\pi)\sqrt{k/\mu}$ recovers mechanical resonance); do not cite this as an independent prediction of C–H frequency.

## Membrane Yield Temperature $T_c \approx 278.3$ K
<!-- id: uowffm -->

- $T_c = E_\text{HB} / (n_\text{coop} \cdot k_B)$ with $n_\text{coop} = 9$
- _Specific Claims_
  - The membrane lattice yield temperature for a **pure** (cholesterol-free) bilayer is $T_c = 0.2158\,\text{eV} / (9 \times 8.617 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{eV/K}) \approx 278.3$ K = $+5.1^\circ$C.
  - This matches the anomalous water density maximum at $+4.0^\circ$C to within $0.40\%$, cross-validating the cooperative amplification number $n_\text{coop} = 9$ (K4 junction mode count: 3 translational + 3 librational + $z/2$ stretch + $z/4$ bend).
  - Cholesterol acts as a topological phase buffer raising the effective yield to $A_\text{yield}^\text{buffered} = 1 + \varphi \approx 1.7405$, pushing $T_c^\text{buffered} \approx 485$ K — outside biological range.
- _Specific Non-Claims and Caveats_
  - The numerical agreement is between membrane $T_c$ and **liquid water density anomaly** $T_\text{max}$ — these are interpretively the same lattice-yield event in the AVE framing, but they are not the same conventional observable. A reader expecting "membrane melting temperature matches measured biological membrane phase transitions" will not find that claim here; the comparison target is water's $4^\circ$C density maximum.
  - $n_\text{coop} = 9$ is "cross-validated" by the agreement, but the K4 junction mode-count derivation lives in Vol 3 Ch.13 (referenced); within Vol 5 it enters as an input from Vol 3.
  - Cholesterol's effective yield $1 + \varphi$ is asserted via the FCC packing-fraction interpretation; the $T_c^\text{buffered} \approx 485$ K is a structural consequence of that assertion, not an independent fit to membrane phase data.
  - Does NOT claim that all cholesterol concentrations rescale the yield by exactly $1 + \varphi$. The leaf treats cholesterol as the limiting buffer; concentration-dependent behaviour is not bounded.

> **Leaf references:** `molecular-foundations/organic-circuitry/membrane-phase-buffering.md`, `molecular-foundations/organic-circuitry/hbond-op4-equilibrium.md`.

## Protein Backbone AC Transceiver Architecture
<!-- id: huhz7r -->

- _Specific Claims_
  - Every amino acid backbone is identified as a three-port AC transceiver: amino-group $\text{NH}_3^+$ as the high-frequency source ($V_\text{sin}$ in SPICE), $\text{C}_\alpha$ as the central transmission node carrying the inductive payload ($L \approx 115.9$ fH), and the $\text{COO}^-$ carboxyl as the phase-locked capacitive sink terminating into the universal vacuum impedance load $Z_0 \approx 376.73\,\Omega$.
  - This source/payload/sink decomposition is the structural template that makes the per-residue SPICE netlists in `simulation-results-zero-parameter.md` and `batch-spice-20-amino-acids.md` well-formed (every chain element has a defined drive, a defined node, and a defined termination).
  - The $Z_0 = \sqrt{\mu_0/\varepsilon_0}$ termination is the same Axiom 1 vacuum impedance used throughout the AVE framework — no biology-specific termination impedance is introduced.
