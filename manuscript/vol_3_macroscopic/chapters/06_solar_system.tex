\label{sec:solar_impedance}

the photosphere to the interstellar medium:
\begin{resultbox}{Heliospheric Impedance Profile}
\begin{equation}

\section{Oort Cloud as Saturation Boundary}
\label{sec:oort_cloud}

The inner boundary of the Oort Cloud corresponds to the radius where
solar gravitational acceleration reaches the MOND threshold:
\begin{resultbox}{Oort Cloud Saturation Boundary}
\begin{equation}
    g_N(r) = \frac{GM_\odot}{r^2} = a_0
    \quad\longrightarrow\quad
    r_{\text{sat}} = \sqrt{\frac{GM_\odot}{a_0}}
    \approx 7{,}400\;\text{AU}
    \label{eq:oort_saturation}
\end{equation}
\end{resultbox}
This falls within the observed Hills Cloud range (2,000--5,000~AU inner edge),
confirming that the Oort Cloud is the solar system's \textit{impedance boundary}.

\section{Planetary Magnetospheres as Impedance Cavities}
\label{sec:planetary_magnetosphere}

Each planet's magnetic dipole field creates a magnetopause:
the boundary where magnetic pressure balances solar wind
dynamic pressure.  In impedance terms:
\begin{resultbox}{Planetary Magnetopause Standoff}
\begin{equation}
    \frac{B^2(r_{mp})}{2\mu_0} = \frac{1}{2}\rho_{sw} v_{sw}^2
    \quad\longrightarrow\quad
    r_{mp} = R_p \left(\frac{B_{eq}^2}{2\mu_0 P_{sw}}\right)^{1/6}
    \label{eq:magnetopause}
\end{equation}
\end{resultbox}
The reflection coefficient $\Gamma$ at the magnetopause is computed
using the same \texttt{reflection\_coefficient()} function as all
other domains.

\subsection{The Plasma Standoff vs. The Gravitational Stator}

(For explicit empirical validation, see the derivation of the Earth Flyby Anomaly as an Acoustic Shear Operator at the planet's solid stator boundary $R_\oplus$ in Section~\ref{sec:flyby_anomaly}).

\textbf{Venus and Mars as the Control Group:} This structural separation is proven definitively by Venus and Mars. Neither planet possesses an active intrinsic dynamo (no global magnetosphere), and thus both suffer aggressive atmospheric stripping precisely because they lack a plasma impedance cavity. Yet, their orbits obey identically precise Topo-Kinematic Keplerian mechanics as Earth and Jupiter. They suffer zero anomalous gravitational friction because the gravitational AC stator anchors exclusively to the atomic mass lattice, operating wholly independent of the magnetic planetary boundaries.
