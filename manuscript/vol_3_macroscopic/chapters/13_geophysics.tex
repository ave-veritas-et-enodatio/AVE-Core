\section{Earth's Core Temperature from Adiabatic LC Compression}
\label{sec:core_temperature}

Standard geophysics derives the inner-core boundary (ICB) temperature from mineral physics extrapolations and shock-wave experiments. The AVE framework derives it from the impedance stiffening of the LC lattice under gravitational self-compression.

\subsection{Derivation}

The key identity is the VCA thermodynamic bridge: temperature is the kinetic energy per degree of freedom of a lattice node, mapped through the Boltzmann conversion constant $k_B$:
\begin{equation}
    T = \frac{\langle E_{\text{kinetic}} \rangle}{k_B}
\end{equation}

For an adiabatically compressed LC lattice, the local kinetic energy density scales with the compressional impedance $Z = \rho V_P$. At the surface, the mean crustal temperature is $T_s \approx 300$ K with impedance $Z_s \approx 15.1 \times 10^6$ Pa$\cdot$s/m. At the ICB, $Z_{ICB} \approx 140.4 \times 10^6$ Pa$\cdot$s/m.

The adiabatic temperature gradient on the LC lattice is governed by the Gr\"uneisen relation. For a lattice with Poisson ratio $\nu_{vac} = 2/7$, the thermodynamic Gr\"uneisen parameter is:
\begin{equation}
    \gamma_G = \frac{3(1 + \nu)}{2(2 - 3\nu)} = \frac{3(1 + 2/7)}{2(2 - 6/7)} = \frac{3 \cdot 9/7}{2 \cdot 8/7} = \frac{27}{16} = 1.6875
    \label{eq:gruneisen}
\end{equation}

The adiabatic temperature ratio between two layers is:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{T_2}{T_1} = \left( \frac{\rho_2}{\rho_1} \right)^{\gamma_G}
\end{equation}

Applying this across the full PREM column from crust ($\rho_s = 2{,}600$ kg/m$^3$) to ICB ($\rho_{ICB} = 12{,}760$ kg/m$^3$):
\begin{equation}
    T_{ICB} = T_s \cdot \left( \frac{\rho_{ICB}}{\rho_s} \right)^{\gamma_G} = 300 \cdot \left( \frac{12{,}760}{2{,}600} \right)^{27/16}
\end{equation}

Evaluating: $(12{,}760 / 2{,}600) = 4.908$, and $4.908^{27/16} = 4.908^{1.6875} \approx 14.65$:
