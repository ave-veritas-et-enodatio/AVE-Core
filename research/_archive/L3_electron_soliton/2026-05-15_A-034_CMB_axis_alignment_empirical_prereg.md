## 2. Background: what's already known observationally

**CMB axis-of-evil (Planck data):**
The CMB's low-ℓ multipoles (quadrupole ℓ=2, octupole ℓ=3) show anomalous
alignment with each other and with the ecliptic plane. Published literature
gives the preferred axis at approximately **(l=174°, b=-5°) in galactic
coordinates**, with significance ~3σ depending on statistic and data
selection. Standard cosmology has no compelling explanation.

**Hubble flow anisotropy:**
Pantheon+ supernova compilations have found ~few-percent-level anisotropy
in $H_0$ measurements depending on sky direction. The "Hubble tension"
between local (Cepheids/SNe) and CMB-inferred values may have an
anisotropic component. Direction of maximum anisotropy has been proposed
but not strongly localized.

**Large-scale-structure rotation:**
SDSS galaxy spin orientation studies have reported weak (~1-2σ) preferred
spin axis directions, but the result is contested and may be selection
bias. Recent JWST data provides additional handles.

**Matter-antimatter asymmetry direction:**
There's no direct "cosmic chirality axis" measurement in standard
cosmology — the asymmetry is observed as a magnitude, not direction. A-034
predicts there SHOULD be a directional aspect inherited from the seed
crystallization event. Indirect probes: CP-violation observations in
distant astrophysical sources, neutrino flavor distributions.

## 3. Methodology for each test (per ave-prereg Step 3)

### 3.1 CMB axis-of-evil characterization

**Data:** Planck 2018 final data release (PR3); high-resolution temperature
and polarization maps.

**Method:** Compute multipole alignment statistic (e.g., axis-of-evil
preferred-axis estimator from de Oliveira-Costa et al. 2004, or modern
refinements). Output: preferred axis direction $(l_{\text{CMB}}, b_{\text{CMB}})$
with 1σ + 3σ uncertainty regions.

**Pre-registered value to compare:** **(l_{CMB}, b_{CMB}) ≈ (174°, -5°)** (literature value).

### 3.2 Hubble flow anisotropy axis

**Data:** Pantheon+ supernova compilation (Brout et al. 2022 or latest).

**Method:** Apply dipole/quadrupole anisotropy decomposition to $H_0$
measurements as a function of sky direction. Extract dipole direction
$(l_H, b_H)$ with uncertainty.

**Pre-registered hypothesis:** $(l_H, b_H)$ should align with $(l_{CMB}, b_{CMB})$
within combined ~10-15° uncertainty (the larger of either survey's
uncertainty regions).

### 3.3 Galaxy spin / large-scale rotation axis

**Data:** SDSS DR16/DR17 spectroscopic galaxy spin orientations + Galaxy
Zoo morphological data; additional handle from JWST high-z surveys if
available.

**Method:** Compute preferred galaxy spin orientation as function of sky
direction; extract dipole/preferred-axis direction $(l_S, b_S)$.

**Pre-registered hypothesis:** $(l_S, b_S)$ aligns with $(l_{CMB}, b_{CMB})$
and $(l_H, b_H)$ within combined uncertainty (~15-20° given SDSS spin
measurement uncertainty).

### 3.4 Matter-asymmetry / CP-violation directional probe

**Data:** distant astrophysical CP-violation observations (e.g.,
high-energy cosmic-ray asymmetries, AGN polarization anomalies), neutrino
flavor distribution anisotropies.

**Method:** Identify whether any of these observables exhibit a preferred
direction, and if so extract direction $(l_M, b_M)$.

**Pre-registered hypothesis:** $(l_M, b_M)$ aligns with the other three
axes; OR (weaker hypothesis), the CMB chirality observable in low-ℓ
B-mode patterns aligns with the other axes.

Note: this is the weakest of the four tests because the directional
probes for matter asymmetry are themselves marginal. May be inconclusive.
