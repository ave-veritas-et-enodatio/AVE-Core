## §1 — One-sentence summary

A single coherent high-purity germanium (HPGe) detector at 9.39 kg, operated underground at the cleanest available background level, observing for 2-3 years in the 2-6 keV energy window, tests **four AVE-distinct dark-sector claims simultaneously** at quantitative precision sufficient to discriminate from Standard Model + Moseley-K-line + cosmic-X-ray-background alternatives.

## §2 — The four AVE-distinct claims under simultaneous test

| Claim | AVE Prediction | Standard Model Prediction |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) Z-INDEPENDENCE of line position** | Line at 3.728 keV in any solid coherent crystal regardless of constituent Z | Moseley K-lines at element-specific energies; Ge native Kα at 9.886 keV, K-edge at 11.10 keV — **ZERO native rate at 3.728 keV** |
| **(b) Rate magnitude** | $R_{predicted} = N_e^{(kg)} \cdot \nu_{slew} \cdot 4\pi/N_{single}^2 \approx 4.96 \times 10^{-7}$ events/s/kg at $\kappa_{quality} = 1$ (theoretical ceiling) | Free per-detector cross-section (WIMP-class fit); or CXB photoabsorption at ~10⁻⁷-10⁻⁸ events/s/kg (comparable OOM but spectrally smooth, not a line) |
| **(c) Annual modulation PHASE** | Peak day-of-year ~152 (early June), matching Earth's CMB-frame velocity maximum | Solar-driven backgrounds peak at perihelion (~day 3, January); cosmogenic backgrounds peak at solar maximum (~11-year cycle) |
| **(d) Solid-vs-liquid binary gate** | Coupling exists only in coherent crystal ($G > 0$); HPGe single-crystal is canonical solid | No solid-vs-liquid dependence in any SM mechanism |

A SINGLE EXPERIMENTAL OBSERVATION confirms or falsifies all four claims. This is the cleanest single-experiment discriminator the framework currently offers.

## §4 — Expected signal and sensitivity

**Predicted signal rate** (per AVE candidate formula, $\kappa_{quality} = 1$):
$$R_{HPGe}^{predicted} = 4.96 \times 10^{-7}\,\text{events/s/kg} \times 9.39\,\text{kg} \times 86400\,\text{s/day} \approx 0.40\,\text{events/day}$$

Annual signal events: $\approx 147 \text{ events/year}$ in the 2-6 keV window.

**Background estimate** (clean HPGe at LNGS-class depth):
- $10^{-3}$ events/keV/kg/day × 4 keV (2-6 keV window) × 9.39 kg × 365 days $\approx 14{,}000$ background events/year
- S/B $\approx 0.01$ broadband; but predicted signal is a discrete LINE at 3.728 keV (within ~50 eV) so spectral fit gains $\sim 100\times$ vs broadband:
- Line-resolved S/B $\approx 1$ at predicted rate (in resolution element near 3.728 keV)

**Statistical sensitivity timeline** (assuming line-shape fit + background subtraction at standard HPGe precision):
- 1 year integration: 3σ detection at $\kappa_{quality} = 1$; 2σ at $\kappa_{quality} = 0.5$
- 2 year integration: 5σ detection at $\kappa_{quality} = 1$; 3σ at $\kappa_{quality} = 0.5$
- 3 year integration: discriminates between $\kappa_{quality}$ values 0.3-1.0 at >2σ

**Annual modulation phase** (claim c): with 2-3 year live time and ~140 signal events/year, the modulation amplitude (~4% per Earth CMB-frame orbital velocity sweep) gives $\sim 6$ excess events in June vs December annually. Phase determination at ~1-month precision requires 3+ year integration.

## §5 — Falsification conditions

The experiment cleanly FALSIFIES the AVE matched-LC-coupling framework if any of:

1. **Falsifier A — No line at 3.728 keV** after 2-year integration at $\geq 2\sigma$ sensitivity (consistent with $\kappa_{quality} = 0$): the AVE substrate-rate coupling to coherent HPGe crystal is null. This would NOT falsify the energy-scale prediction (3.728 keV may still be in DAMA NaI from Ca contamination per Moseley), but it WOULD falsify the Z-independence + matched-LC-coupling framework.

2. **Falsifier B — Line observed but at $\kappa$-corrected rate inconsistent with $\kappa_{quality} \in [0, 1]$** (e.g., rate $> 1.03 \times$ DAMA rate/kg, requiring $\kappa_{quality} > 1$): the $4\pi/N_{single}^2$ formula is wrong; rate scales by something other than $N_{single}^2$.

3. **Falsifier C — Modulation peak shifted from day-of-year ~152** (e.g., perihelion-aligned or no modulation at all): the CMB-velocity phase-lock claim fails. Specifically, peak at day-of-year $3 \pm 30$ would indicate solar-driven mechanism; null modulation would indicate the substrate-rate signal is not Earth-velocity-coupled.

4. **Falsifier D — Line observed at element-specific Moseley energies but NOT at 3.728 keV** (e.g., Ge Kα at 9.886 keV stronger than 3.728 keV, after background subtraction): the AVE substrate-rate framing is wrong; the DAMA signal is plausibly Ca contamination in NaI specifically.

The experiment CONFIRMS the AVE matched-LC-coupling framework if:

- Line observed at $3.728 \pm 0.05$ keV with rate consistent with $\kappa_{quality}$ between 0.5 and 1.5 of the theoretical ceiling
- Annual modulation peak observed at day-of-year $152 \pm 30$
- No anomalous line at Ge native K-lines beyond expected background

## §6 — Why HPGe specifically (vs Sapphire, CaF₂, other alternatives)

| Alternative | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **HPGe (chosen)** | Mature low-background technology; commercial 5-15 kg crystals; sub-keV resolution; no native K-line near 3.7 keV | Cryogenic operation (77 K); Ge K-edges at 11 keV inside detection range |
| Sapphire (Al₂O₃) | Higher Debye temperature (longer phonon coherence?); transparent (optical readout possible) | No commercial single-crystal mass > 1 kg at scintillator-grade purity; would require ~3 kg matched-N detector with significant infrastructure investment |
| CaF₂ | Scintillator-grade large crystals available | Contains Ca → **same Moseley Kα anti-anchor as NaI**, defeats Z-independence test |
| Liquid Ar or Xe | Massive (multi-ton) detectors operational | **Liquids fail solid-vs-liquid binary gate** — AVE predicts null, so liquid Ar/Xe is the CONTROL experiment (already in XENONnT null), not the test |

**HPGe is uniquely positioned** to test all four AVE-distinct claims simultaneously with mature technology and no atomic-physics anti-anchor in the 3.728 keV region.
